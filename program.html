  • 11:15-12:15
    Keynote. "Quantum Service Oriented Computing. Current Landscape and Challenges"

    Juan Manuel Murillo

    Professor of Advanced Software Engineering Techniques at the University of Extremadura.

    The last two decades have meant a radical change in the way information systems are built and consumed. Indeed, the development of the Internet and the cloud computing paradigm has allowed developers to create information systems at different service levels: infrastructure (IaaS), platform (PaaS) and software (SaaS). Service Oriented Computing (SOC) has enabled the construction of systems based on the composition of services deployed at different levels.

Session 2. 13:00 - 14:30

  • 13:00-13:30
    QuantumPath´s Annealer Compositor

    Jose Luis Hevia, Guido Peterssen, Ezequiel Murina, Aurelio Martínez and Mario Piattini

    Among the various challenges that quantum computing faces to become a widespread reality are, on the one hand, the need to research and apply the existing software engineering processes; and on the other hand, the shortage of specialized workforce. QPath® is a tool that solves these two challenges, in addition to presenting other advantages for the development of hybrid information systems. In this paper we present the Annealer Compositor that facilitates the definition and execution of annealing algorithms in either quantum annealing or digital annealing computers.

  • 13:30-14:00
    Cybersecurity for Quantum Computing

    Natalie Kilber

    Cybersecurity is preparing for what is next, yet it is often an afterthought. With the regularity of security breaches and the rising cyberattack frequency and range impacting academia and especially, an array of industries, Quantum Computing groups, companies and institutions may become targets for nation-state actors, APTs or cybercriminals for ransomware and data theft as the Quantum computing race intensifies. It is an imperative to build in security during the design phase and therefore shift security left to ensure Quantum Computing ecosystems, services, as well as promising technologies are reliable and secure to use. This session will bring you up-to-speed on the field of cybersecurity, including types of cyberattacks and it’s growing global importance in a digital world bridging from research to industry. This workshop raises questions on how to secure the future of Quantum Software Engineering

  • 14:00-14:30
    Software Quality Issues in Quantum Information Systems

    Javier Verdugo, Moisés Rodríguez and Mario Piattini

    Quantum computing is the technology of the 21st century. Quantum computers and environments are already offering great advantages when building advanced applications in finance, health, or logistics. However, if industry is to boost the large-scale production of quantum software, an adequate quality level must be achieved and assured. In this sense, it is very important to consider quantum software quality platforms and products, and to create an effective quality environment for quantum software. This paper summarises some of these issues.

Session 3. 15:15 - 16:45

  • 15:15-15:45
    Constraints for executing quantum programs in real quantum devices

    Lorenzo Saraiva, Edward Hermann Haeusler, Vaston Costa and Marcos Kalinowski

    Quantum software engineering is still a new area, with different amount of research dedicated to each subarea, with very little for requirements reasearch. We'll cover some low level constraints that have to be taken into account when developing quantum programs for real quantum devices that can be potentially translated into non-functional requirements. Then, we'll proceed to review the different approaches for high level abtractions that deal with these constraints when executing on Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum Computing (NISQs), such as quantum volume, quantum provenance, compilers and transpilers.

  • 15:45-16:15
    Formal Quantum Software Engineering

    Carmelo R. Cartiere

    This talk introduces the formal methods of software engineering to quantum computing.

  • 16:15-16:45
    Testing Quantum Programs using Q# and Microsoft Quantum Development Kit

    and Mathias Soeken

    As the quantum software matures, the quantum codebases grow in size and complexity, and so does the task of testing them and verifying their correctness. In this session we’ll show how to test and validate several common types of quantum programs written in the quantum programming language Q# using the tools provided by the Microsoft Quantum Development Kit. Our approach uses multiple simulators and library tools for program testing and resource estimation, performing these steps before running the programs on quantum hardware. The demonstrated approach is illustrated with code examples from the Quantum Katas, a collection of quantum programming tutorials that provide immediate feedback for the learner’s solution.
